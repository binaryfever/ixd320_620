Why do marathon runners speed up in the last mile?

Their legs are more tired, not less. But the finish line is visible.

Motivation rises as the finish line gets closer.

Kivetz, Urminsky and Zheng, 2006. Customers bought coffee faster as they approached the free reward. Clean, replicated, engineerable.

Situation. A learner, between classes, with a goal that's years away.

Fluency in a new language is a finish line you can never actually see.

Duolingo's question: how do you keep someone motivated when the real goal is that far out?

Decision. Make progress visible at three scales, at once.

Daily XP. Today's goal. Closes in one session.

Streak. Consecutive days. Closes nightly.

League. Weekly ranking against other learners. Closes in seven days.

There's always a goal you are close to.

How to design for it. Three moves.

Make the goal specific and measurable.

Show progress visually. A bar, a ring, a filled shape.

Break the journey into near milestones, and recognise each one.

Part two. Measuring the work.

If the goal gradient is how we move people toward a finish line, UX metrics are how we see whether they're getting there.

A quantitative signal about the user's experience.

Not revenue. Not monthly actives. Metrics that sit close to the interaction itself. What happens in the moment of use.

Five you'll see over and over

Each one answers a different question.

01 · Task success

Did they finish?

Percent of users who complete the task. The baseline question of usability.

02 · Error rate

How often did they slip?

Mistakes per task. High error rates point to a usability defect.

03 · Time on task

How long did it take?

Shorter is usually better. Longer is friction worth investigating.

04 · Satisfaction

How did it feel?

Collected via survey. Subjective, but often the earliest warning sign.

05 · NPS

Would they tell a friend?

One question, eleven-point scale. Loved and loathed in equal measure.

Custom metrics

The five are general. Your pattern is specific.

01 · Milestone completion

Did they close the near goal?

For Duolingo: what percent of opens end with today's XP bar full?

02 · Time between wins

How fast do they return?

If the streak is doing work, the gap between sessions shrinks as the next milestone gets closer.

03 · Delta without the signal

Does the pattern actually move them?

Hide the progress bar in a test cell. The difference in completion rate is the metric.

If you can't say what question the metric answers, it's the wrong metric.

Don't use vanity metrics.
Vanity

Looks impressive.

Page views. Signups on their own. Followers. Moves up and to the right. Tells you nothing about what to change next.

Actionable

Tells you where to look.

Task success rate, error rate, funnel drop-off. When the number moves, you know which screen or flow to open.

A single reading tells you nothing. The value is in the trend.
