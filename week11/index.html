Conversation Design
Conversation design is vast.

It covers everything from UI microcopy to voice assistants. Today we focus on text and verbal conversation design: the patterns that make digital interactions feel natural.

The Rise
AI-Powered
Conversations

The rise of AI-powered chatbots and voice assistants demands a focus on designing user-friendly conversational interfaces to meet growing expectations for natural interactions.

Conversation Design
Definition & Goal
Definition
What it is
The process of creating natural and engaging voice or text interactions between users and conversational interfaces.
Goal
What it achieves
Understanding intent (with AI), clarity, efficiency, and personality in every interaction.
Conversation Design
Design Principles
  • 01Clarity: Responses are straightforward and to the point, minimizing confusion.
  • 02Natural Language: Use everyday language that resonates with users, not system speak.
  • 03Guidance: Guide users through the conversation by clearly outlining what they can expect next.
  • 04Error Recovery: Anticipate misunderstandings and provide helpful recovery options to keep the conversation on track.
Conversation Design
Design Principles continued
  • 05Personalization: Tailor interactions based on user preferences and history when possible, making the experience feel more relevant.
  • 06Consistent Voice: Maintain a uniform voice throughout interactions, building trust and familiarity with users.
Conversation Design
Challenges
  • 01Capturing Intent: Understanding exactly what the user wants is crucial. AI is increasingly solving this at scale.
  • 02Unpredictable Input: Users express themselves in countless ways: "yes," "yeah," "mhhmm," "yep."
  • 03Dead Ends: Conversations that hit a wall cause users to abandon the interaction entirely.

Conversation Design
Creating a Conversation
  • 01Identify user intents: Map what users want to accomplish and what they expect in return.
  • 02Create a system persona: Define your AI's voice and character, then write a script with that voice.
  • 03Role-play the scenario: Rehearse with a teammate to surface gaps and awkward moments before testing.
  • 04Build in alternatives: Include varied phrasing to handle different user responses and maintain engagement.

User Testing
The Wizard of Oz Method
  • 01No system required: Test your conversation design with a real user without building the system first. A teammate plays the AI.
  • 02Real-time response: The "wizard" responds as the system would, revealing gaps and awkward moments the script missed.
  • 03Authentic reactions: The user is not aware a human is behind the curtain, making their responses genuine.

Notification Design
Principles
  • 01Timely & relevant: Notifications serve to deliver the right information at the right moment.
  • 02Balance value with disruption: Useful information must be weighed against the cost of interruption.
  • 03Poor design has real consequences: Annoyance, decreased productivity, and app uninstalls all follow bad notification design.
Notification Design
Types of Notifications
System
OS-level alerts
Battery, updates, connectivity. High trust. Users expect these to be essential.
In-App
While app is open
Real-time updates: chat messages, status changes, progress. User is already engaged.
Push
App is closed
Alerts sent to the device even when the app isn't open. Used for reminders and re-engagement.
Notification Design
Types of Notifications continued
Badges
Passive indicators
A number or dot on an app icon showing unread activity. Only seen when the user looks at the home screen.
SMS
Text messages
High-priority text alerts: verification codes, appointment reminders, delivery updates.

Notification Design
Avoid Notification Bursts

A flood of simultaneous notifications creates high cognitive load, leading to frustration, annoyance, and notification fatigue.

Batch and pace notifications strategically. The right cadence feels helpful; the wrong one gets you uninstalled.

Notification Design
Consider Time & Location
  • 01Time of day: Don't push at 2am. Use scheduled delivery and quiet hours.
  • 02User state: Is the user in Do Not Disturb? Driving? In a meeting?
  • 03Location context: Geo-triggered notifications should feel genuinely helpful, not like surveillance.
Notification Design
Give Users Control
  • 01Granular controls: Per notification type and category, not just a single on/off switch.
  • 02Frequency & timing: Let users decide when they want to hear from you.
  • 03Channel preferences: Push, email, SMS, in-app. Not everyone wants the same channel.
