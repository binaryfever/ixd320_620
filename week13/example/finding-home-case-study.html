The person at the centre of everything

Maya wasn't invented. She's a composite of 24 research participants — young adults making a high-stakes decision in a system designed to move inventory, not help them feel confident.

What she says she wants

"Better search filters. More listings."

What she actually needs

Confidence. A signal that says this one is right for you.

The gap we had to close

More information was making her more anxious, not less.

In their own words
"I visited a place I loved. Then I saw one Reddit comment about rats. I didn't take it. Six months later I still wonder if I made the right call."
— Marcus, 26, software engineer
"I wish someone who had already done this could walk me through it. Not a landlord. Not an algorithm. A person."
— Leila, 23, designer
"I did 11 viewings in 2 days. I cried in my car between the 7th and 8th. My partner thought I was being dramatic."
— James, 29, teacher
Three things that kept coming up

No matter who we talked to.

Trust is broken.

Every platform has an incentive to mislead — photos, descriptions, reviews — all curated for the landlord, not the renter.

It's a feeling, not a transaction.

People need to know: who lives here? will I fit? No app was answering that.

Time doesn't help. It hurts.

Longer searches don't yield better decisions. Open-ended searching actively makes people choose worse options.

The turn
They're not looking for more listings.
They're looking for permission to stop searching.

The problem was never discoverability. It was the confidence to commit.

The moment the pattern becomes clear
A moment of honesty

We built the wrong thing first.

Version one

A smart filter system — 23 parameters, AI-ranked results, hyper-personalised. We were proud of it. Technically impressive. Users said:

"This is just… more listings."

What testing showed us
  • !
    Users skipped all 23 filters and went straight to photos
  • !
    More parameters increased anxiety, not confidence
  • !
    The one thing they lingered on? A resident quote we'd added as throwaway filler text

We killed six weeks of work and pivoted to human signals over algorithmic ranking.

What we'd carry into every project after this
🔍

The real problem is almost never the stated problem.

Users asked for "better search". They needed confidence. Stay curious about why, not just what.

💔

Throwing away 6 weeks of work was the bravest design decision we made.

The sunk cost pressure was real. Honouring what users told us built something worth making.

🧠

Emotion is not soft data. It is primary data.

Maya's 12:47 AM wasn't a detail. It was the brief. Treat emotion as signal, never noise.

📐

Simple beats sophisticated every time.

23-parameter AI filter vs. one human voice saying "I've lived here 8 months and I feel safe." The voice won.
